Leather watch straps

Leather straps are comfortable to wear and provide a sleek, elegant appearance. Stylistically they suit almost any watch.

We we love them?

Let's be honest, we love leather straps because they look great, especially after few months of wear when they conform to the shape of the wrist. Plus they compliment a variety of outfits and types of watches.

  • Unmatched number of patterns and finishes
  • They look better with age
  • Good ones are very comfortable
  • Stylistically they suit almost any watch
  • "It doesn't get serious until it's on leather!"

Average price of a leather strap

Price of a leather watch strap can vary widely depending on the type of leather & the brand. But it is safe to say that affordable straps are priced between $20 and $50, really good ones from $50 to $80, and the high-qualily straps start from $80.

An entry point for full-grain handmade stuff is usually at $80, but be aware that prices go up fairly quickly from there.

Common types of leather straps

A broad categorisation that covers almost 99% of all needs an average watch enhusiast might have can look like this:

  • Two piece leather straps
  • Leather natos
  • Bund straps


What about the leaher?

When it comes to leather, watch straps are usually made from following varieties of leather:

Full grain leather: This is the highest quality leather that is made from the top layer of the hide. It is known for its durability and resistance to wear and tear.

Top grain leather: This is a slightly lower quality leather that is made from the second layer of the hide. It is less durable than full grain leather, but it is still considered a high-quality material.

Genuine leather: This is a lower quality leather that is made from the inner layers of the hide. It is not as durable as full grain or top grain leather and is used in lower-priced bands.

Bonded leather: This is a synthetic leather material that is made from leftover pieces of leather that are bonded together.

Suede leather: This is a type of leather that has a soft, velvety finish. It is often used in dressier watch straps and can be prone to staining and scuffing.
