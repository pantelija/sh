How to change a watch strap without a spring bar tool?

In this short article I will show you a simple way to remove and change a watch strap without a spring bar tool.

You will need a knife. I use a pocket knife with a thin blade.

If you look at what a spring bar looks like, you will see that these little gadgets have small shoulders on the side. These shoulders can be retracted and thus freed from the lug holes.

Our task is to move these shoulders with the blade of the knife.

Here's the procedure.

  1. Take the watch in one hand and fold the strap so it doesn't get in the way.
  2. Carefully insert the blade between the inside of the lug and the strap until you feel that the blade has reached the spring bar.
  3. Position the blade so that it catches the shoulder of the spring bar.
  4. Rotate the blade slightly to release the spring bar.
  5. That's it, the strap will be removed. Do the same on the other side.

Closing thoughts

This process is even faster than using the spring bar tool. Of course, be extra careful. Do not use a very sharp knife. The direction of a blade should always go away from you.

In this way, you can remove leather and rubber straps, while you still need a specific tool for changing stainless steel bracelets.

To put the strap back, do the following: Insert one side of the spring bar into the lug hole. Then retract the other side of the spring bar, slide the strap end under the lug and gently move it until you hear a clicking sound. This is a sign that the spring has snapped back into the lug hole and that the strap is secured.
